# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Lumenfold is for self-directed learners, including lifelong learners and students, who want to build durable understanding across subjects and projects. They may arrive with scattered sources, incomplete notes, or an unclear study process. They need a workspace that supports repeated learning without prescribing one rigid method or demanding constant attention from the interface.

## Product Purpose

Lumenfold supports learning as a continuing cycle: collect and organize sources, distill and understand useful ideas, review and retrieve them, connect them with existing knowledge, and plan what to explore next. Success is not a one-time AI output. It is a growing body of learner-owned notes and understanding that becomes easier to retrieve, apply, and extend over time.

The hackathon MVP demonstrates this cycle in a responsive browser application. The longer-term product is intended to become a locally installed, local-first application while preserving portable, human-readable files.

## Positioning

Lumenfold is a continuous learning system that carries knowledge from collection through understanding, retrieval, and connection.

## Assistant Role

The assistant is a capable collaborator across the learning cycle. It can answer questions, summarize and explain sources, search and sort the vault, capture references, design quizzes and other instruments, and draft reusable skills or plugin configurations. Its role is to reduce setup and retrieval friction while leaving judgment and authorship with the learner.

The assistant may prepare a durable change, but every write is a proposal with a clear preview or diff. The learner approves what is saved, and the workspace never grants the model unrestricted database or filesystem control. Provider credentials belong to the deployment or local secret store rather than the portable vault.

## Brand Personality

The product is calm, clear, empowering, and friendly. It communicates through capable behavior and restrained language rather than slogans, praise, or interruption. The introduction may be more ambitious and inspiring, but the workspace remains modest and direct.

## Anti-references

Lumenfold must not resemble products that treat learners as passive or incapable. Avoid forced tours, disposable onboarding checklists, motivational nags, celebratory interruptions, chatty controls, hidden essential functions, and one-time UI that becomes clutter after first use. Avoid turning the workspace into a marketing surface or overwhelming learners with the product's philosophy while they are trying to work.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice silent competence.** Expose real objects and useful actions, provide sensible defaults, and trust learners to develop their own working habits.
2. **Make the learning cycle legible.** Sources, notes, instruments, connections, and planning should remain distinct while clearly belonging to one continuing process.
3. **Keep the learner in control.** AI proposes and explains; the learner decides what deserves attention and what becomes a durable note or instrument.
4. **Let the product prove the vision.** The introduction can express the ambition, but the working demo must make the promise credible through a complete, understandable workflow.
5. **Preserve ownership.** Portable content and transparent structure are product behavior, not merely an export feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The hackathon MVP prioritizes readable contrast, keyboard access through the core workflow, visible focus states, reduced-motion handling, and status communication that does not rely on color alone. WCAG 2.2 AA conformance, broader reading controls, and exhaustive accessibility testing remain long-term product requirements after the MVP.
